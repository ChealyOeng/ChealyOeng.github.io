Project Report

Synthetic fibres have been widely used in the fashion industry due to their ability to create various fabric textures and mimic natural fibres at a lower cost. These materials have been proven to be unsustainable due to their non-biodegradability and the harm that they pose to the environment and our health. This report will explore the current production landscape of some of the most sustainable natural fibre or material options available in the industry. These include organic cotton, linen, silk, regenerative wool, and bacterial cellulose. The opportunities identified can help increase efficiency in fibre production in order to make them more affordable and accessible to consumers.
